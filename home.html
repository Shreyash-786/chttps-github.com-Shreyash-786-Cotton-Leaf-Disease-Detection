Introduction

Farmers need automatic disease monitoring of plants rather than human monitoring to increase crop field growth and yield. Manual disease monitoring is poor because it requires an expert to recognize disease, which takes longer and demands more time, making it ineffective. As a result, this research introduced a new technique for detecting illness in both leaves. A computer image processing technique was utilized to identify plant disease rapidly and effectively, overcoming the limits of traditional visual observation. Using a k-means clustering method and a multi SVM technique, we developed software for disease diagnosis and classification in our suggested study. This suggested method gives an overview of how machine learning might be used to classify and identify plant leaf diseases. Three- or more-layer neural networks are one subclass of machine learning approaches based on an artificial neuron model.

About The Project

The aim for developing enhanced cotton leaf disease identification systems using automated image processing is primarily driven by the significant challenges faced by the agricultural sector, particularly in cotton cultivation. By addressing these challenges and leveraging the potential of automated image processing, researchers and developers aim to create tools that empower farmers to protect their crops, increase yields, and improve overall agricultural sustainability
